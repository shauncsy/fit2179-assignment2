NBL vs NBA Basketball Visualisation

This data visualisation project compares the Australian National Basketball League with the NBA, focusing on teams, locations, player performance, and league development.

Project Introduction

Basketball is becoming increasingly popular in Australia. This project uses interactive visualisations to explore how the NBL compares with the NBA in terms of geography, team structure, and player performance.

Visualisation 1: NBL Team Locations

This map shows NBL team locations across Australia and New Zealand using the prepared NBL team statistics dataset. The number inside each point represents the team's calculated ranking, while the colour represents win percentage. Users can hover over each point to view team performance details such as wins, losses, shooting percentage, rebounds, assists, and turnovers.

Visualisation 2: NBL Offensive and Defensive Performance Map

This map explores NBL team performance from offensive and defensive perspectives. Users can switch between offensive score, defensive score against, and score difference. This helps compare not only where teams are located, but also how they perform on court.

Section 2: NBL Team Statistical Analysis

This section uses the NBL team statistics dataset to compare teams beyond their geographic locations. It focuses on ranking teams by key performance indicators such as win percentage, score, shooting efficiency, rebounds, and assists.

Visualisation 3: NBL Team Metric Ranking

This interactive bar chart ranks NBL teams by different performance metrics. Users can switch between win percentage, average score, field goal percentage, three-point percentage, rebounds, assists, and turnovers to compare team strengths from different perspectives.

Visualisation 4: NBL Offensive vs Defensive Efficiency

This scatter plot compares each NBL team's offensive and defensive performance. The x-axis shows average score, while the y-axis shows average score against. Teams further to the right have stronger scoring ability, while teams lower on the chart allow fewer points. Circle colour represents win percentage, and circle size represents total rebounds.

This chart shows that teams with higher scoring and lower opponent scoring usually perform better. Sydney is a strong example because it scores highly and also allows fewer points. Brisbane and Cairns are weaker because they score less and allow more opponent points. Overall, stronger NBL teams need both good offense and good defense.

Visualisation 5: NBL Team Performance Profile Heatmap

This heatmap compares NBL teams across multiple performance indicators, including win percentage, scoring, shooting efficiency, rebounds, assists, turnovers, and defensive score against. Darker colours indicate stronger relative performance. For score against and turnovers, lower values are treated as better performance.

This heatmap shows that Sydney Kings and South East Melbourne Phoenix perform strongly across many key metrics. They have darker colours in several areas, especially win percentage, scoring, assists, and turnovers. Brisbane Bullets and Cairns Taipans have lighter colours in most metrics, which shows weaker overall performance. This chart makes it easier to compare each team's strengths and weaknesses across multiple statistics at once.

Section 3: NBL Player Performance Analysis

This section uses NBL player-level statistics to analyse individual scoring style and advanced performance. Unlike the previous section, which focused on team-level comparison, this section focuses on how individual players contribute through scoring, efficiency, and overall impact.

Visualisation 6: NBL Top Player Ranking

This interactive bar chart ranks the top NBL players by selected performance metrics. Users can switch between scoring, assists, rebounds, efficiency, and advanced impact indicators. This provides a more direct ranking view compared with the previous scatter and bubble charts.

Visualisation 7: NBL Player Scoring Style Analysis

This scatter plot compares NBL players by three-point attempts and free throw attempts. Circle size represents points per game, while colour represents effective field goal percentage. Players with at least 10 matches and 10 minutes per game are shown to reduce visual clutter.

This chart shows different scoring styles among NBL players. Some players rely more on three-point shots, while others get more free throw attempts.

Players on the right side take more three-point shots. Players higher on the chart get more free throw chances. Larger circles show players who score more points per game.

Overall, the chart shows that NBL scorers do not all score in the same way. Some are perimeter shooters, while others attack the basket and draw fouls.

Visualisation 8: NBL Player Advanced Efficiency Analysis

This bubble chart compares NBL players using advanced statistics. The x-axis shows PPP, the y-axis shows PIE, circle size represents FIC, and colour represents effective field goal percentage. This helps identify players who combine scoring efficiency, impact, and overall contribution.

This chart shows that players with higher PPP usually also have higher PIE. This means efficient scorers often have stronger overall impact.

Larger circles represent players with higher FIC, so these players contribute more overall. Players in the upper-right area are the strongest because they combine scoring efficiency, player impact, and overall contribution.

Section 4: NBL vs NBA Comparative Analysis

This section compares the NBL and NBA using both team-level and player-level data. It focuses on scoring level, player scoring style, and scoring efficiency. Since the NBL data contains team statistics and player statistics, while the NBA data mainly contains player statistics, NBA team-level scoring is estimated from player total points.

Visualisation 9: NBL vs NBA Team Scoring Level

This chart compares team scoring levels between the NBL and NBA. NBL scoring uses the official team average score from the team dataset, while NBA team scoring is estimated by summing player total points by team and dividing by an 82-game season.

This chart shows that NBA teams generally score more points per game than NBL teams. Most NBA teams are above 100 points per game, while NBL teams are mostly below 100.

This difference suggests that the NBA has a faster and higher-scoring style of play. The NBL scoring level is lower, but there is still variation between teams.

Visualisation 10: NBL vs NBA Player Scoring Style

This scatter plot compares player scoring style in the NBL and NBA. The x-axis shows three-point attempts per game, while the y-axis shows free throw attempts per game. Circle size represents points per game. This helps compare whether players rely more on perimeter shooting, foul drawing, or overall scoring volume.

This chart shows that NBA players generally take more three-point shots and free throws than NBL players. NBA players are more spread out across the chart, showing a wider range of scoring styles.

Most NBL players are closer to the lower-left area, meaning they usually have lower three-point attempts and free throw attempts. Overall, the NBA appears to have more high-volume scorers, while the NBL has a more compact scoring style.

Visualisation 11: NBL vs NBA Player Scoring Efficiency

This scatter plot compares player scoring volume and shooting efficiency. The x-axis shows effective field goal percentage, while the y-axis shows points per game. Circle size represents minutes per game. This helps compare whether high-scoring players are also efficient scorers.

This chart shows that NBA players generally have higher scoring volume than NBL players. Many NBA players score above 20 points per game, while most NBL players are below that level.

Both leagues have players with good shooting efficiency, but NBA players are more spread out. This suggests that the NBA has more high-volume scorers, while the NBL has a smaller group of high-scoring players.
